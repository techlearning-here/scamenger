# Country expansion plan (March 2026)

Scam Avenger currently covers **United States** only. This document recommends which countries to add next based on **internet traffic potential**, **scam/fraud relevance**, and **feasibility** (official reporting channels, language fit).

---

## Data context (2025–2026)

- **Internet users:** ~6 billion globally; top countries by users: China, India, USA, Indonesia, Brazil, Russia, Pakistan, Mexico, Japan, Nigeria.
- **Scam impact:** Victimization and losses are rising everywhere; underreporting is high. Countries with strong reporting infrastructure and high internet use are the best candidates for a directory like this.
- **Language:** The site is English-first; English-speaking or high-English-usage countries are easier to support first.

---

## Recommended next countries (in priority order)

### Tier 1 – High traffic + strong fit (add next)

| Country        | Rationale |
|----------------|-----------|
| **United Kingdom** | High internet use, English, strong reporting (Action Fraud, FCA, National Cyber Security Centre, Trading Standards). High scam awareness and victimization. |
| **Canada**         | English (and French), similar structure to US; Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre (CAFC), provincial consumer protection, FCAC for financial. Large internet user base. |
| **Australia**      | English, Scamwatch (ACCC), IDCARE, ReportCyber; very high scam victimization and strong public awareness. |

### Tier 2 – High traffic or high need (plan after Tier 1)

| Country        | Rationale |
|----------------|-----------|
| **India**      | Very large number of internet users (~800M+), English widely used; CERT-In, consumer commissions, RBI for financial. High fraud volume and need for clear “where to report” guidance. |
| **Germany**    | Large economy and internet base; EU consumer protection, BSI for cyber, BaFin for financial. Many users comfortable with English for this kind of content. |
| **South Africa** | English, high fraud impact relative to economy; NPA, FIC, provincial consumer bodies. Good fit for a dedicated “where to report” directory. |
| **Ireland**    | English, EU consumer and data-protection rules; CCPC, Garda, DPC. Smaller market but straightforward to add. |

### Tier 3 – Large reach, localization or research needed

| Country     | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Brazil**  | Very large internet user base; Portuguese required. ANPD, consumer agencies, BACEN. Add when ready for Portuguese or a dedicated BR version. |
| **Mexico**  | Large user base; Spanish. CONDUSEF, PROFECO, cyber units. Add when ready for Spanish or MX-focused content. |
| **Philippines** | High internet use, English common; BSP, NPC, NBI. Strong potential once local reporting routes are mapped. |
| **Indonesia** | Very large user base; Indonesian (and some English). OJK, BSSN, consumer bodies. Needs localization plan. |
| **Nigeria**  | Large user base; EFCC, NCC, consumer commission. Important for victim reporting; content must be clear and well-sourced. |
| **Pakistan** | Large user base; FIA, PTA, State Bank. High scam impact; needs careful mapping of official channels. |

### Tier 4 – Consider later

- **France** (French, EU – DGCCRF, ANSSI).  
- **Spain** (Spanish, EU – consumer and cyber bodies).  
- **Japan** (Japanese – consumer affairs, cyber police).  
- **Singapore** (English – small but high penetration; CAD, MAS).  
- **New Zealand** (English – Netsafe, ComCom; smaller market).  

---

## Suggested implementation order

1. **UK** – Same language, clear official reporting structure, high traffic and scam relevance.  
2. **Canada** – Same language, CAFC + provincial/FCAC; natural extension of US content.  
3. **Australia** – Same language, Scamwatch/ReportCyber; very high scam awareness.  
4. **India** – Huge traffic, English; requires mapping CERT-In, consumer, and financial reporting.  
5. **Germany** – Large EU market; can start with English, consider DE later.  
6. **South Africa** – English; high fraud impact and need for a simple directory.  

---

## Notes

- **China** has the most users but would require a separate product and compliance approach; not recommended as an early expansion.  
- **USA** is already live; this plan focuses on “next” countries.  
- For each new country, document: national fraud/consumer authority, cyber reporting, financial regulator, and data-protection or consumer complaint portals before building pages.  
- “More countries may be added in the future” on the index page can be updated as each country is launched.
