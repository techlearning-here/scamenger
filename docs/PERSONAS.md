# Scam Avenger — User personas

This document describes the main **personas** who use the site and **how each one uses it**. Use it for homepage structure, navigation, content, and feature prioritization.

---

## Overview

| # | Persona | Primary goal |
|---|----------|--------------|
| 1 | Learner | Learn about different types of scams |
| 2 | Victim reporter | Report a scam and warn others |
| 3 | Protector | Protect devices and accounts using tools and tips |
| 4 | News seeker | Stay updated on latest scam news and be prepared |
| 5 | Victim (emotional support) | Get emotional support from professionals |
| 6 | Family/friend of victim | Help or protect someone close who is (or might be) scammed |
| 7 | Skeptic / verifier | Check if something is a scam before engaging |
| 8 | Recovery-focused victim | Take concrete next steps after being scammed |
| 9 | Business / professional | Protect business and clients from fraud |
| 10 | Educator / community leader | Teach others or share trusted resources |
| 11 | Advocate / return visitor | Stay involved and give back |

## 1. Learner

**Who they are:** Someone keen to learn about different types of scams—how they work, red flags, and how to avoid them. May be curious, proactive, or preparing for a talk or conversation.

**Goals:**
- Understand common scam types (romance, phishing, investment, job, etc.)
- Recognize red flags and patterns
- Know where to report and get help (for themselves or to advise others)

**How they use the website:**
- **Homepage** — Browse “Popular scam & fraud guides” and “Top scams at a glance”; click into categories.
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` (e.g. romance, phishing, identity-theft). Read what the scam is, how it works, where to report, FAQs, related scams.
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Read real experiences to see how scams play out; filter by category.
- **Country topics** — Homepage or topic list: explore guides by category (USA focus; other regions via Help now).
- **Help now** — `/help-now/` — Find official reporting and support links by country when they need to refer someone or themselves.

**Key entry points:** Homepage “Popular guides,” Stories, nav “Stories,” scam type links from homepage.

---

## 2. Victim reporter

**Who they are:** A scam victim who wants to report what happened and warn others so the same thing doesn’t happen to someone else.

**Goals:**
- Submit a report (anonymously, no sign-up)
- Get a shareable link to warn others
- Optionally consent to share with authorities or (when enabled) social channels

**How they use the website:**
- **Report a scam** — `/report/` — Fill the form: country of origin, report type (website, phone, crypto, IBAN, etc.), category, “Lost money?”, narrative, consent. Submit and receive a unique report URL.
- **Look up report** — `/lookup-report/` — Find their report by ID or email if they saved it.
- **View report** — `/reports/?id=...` — Open their report to share the link (e.g. social, WhatsApp).
- **Homepage / nav** — “Report a scam” CTA in hero, nav, and FAB (when enabled).

**Key entry points:** Nav “Report a scam,” hero “sharing your experience,” footer or in-content “Report a scam” links.

---

## 3. Protector

**Who they are:** Someone who wants to protect their devices, accounts, and identity using practical tools and tips—before anything happens.

**Goals:**
- Find actionable tips to secure phone, laptop, bank account, credit card
- Discover trusted tools (official and third-party) by country
- Get reading and resources (e.g. books, financial literacy)

**How they use the website:**
- **Tools** — `/tools/` — Main tools page: protect & avoid, recover, identity & credit; country selector (US, UK, etc.) for official links.
- **Protect pages** — `/tools/protect-phone/`, `/tools/protect-laptop/`, `/tools/protect-bank-account/`, `/tools/protect-credit-card/` — Step-by-step guidance and settings.
- **Books** — `/tools/books/` — Recommended reading for financial literacy and fraud awareness.
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` — “Where to report” and prevention tips per scam type.
- **Homepage** — Hero “protect yourself” → Tools; mission block links to Help now and Immediate help.

**Key entry points:** Nav “Tools,” hero “protect yourself,” links from scam guides.

---

## 4. News seeker

**Who they are:** Someone who wants to stay updated on the latest scam news, trends, and alerts so they can stay prepared.

**Goals:**
- Get scam alerts and new guides in their inbox
- Read latest news or updates (when available)
- Stay aware of new tactics and warnings

**How they use the website:**
- **Newsletter** — `/newsletter/` — Subscribe (email, optional name, topic/frequency); receive digests or alerts (when sent).
- **Homepage** — “Get scam alerts and new guides” CTA block → Newsletter.
- **News** — `/news/` — Browse latest news or PSA-style content (when populated).
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Real stories as a form of “what’s happening”; RSS feed for “latest” when available.

**Key entry points:** Homepage newsletter CTA, nav (if linked), footer “Newsletter.”

---

## 5. Victim (emotional support)

**Who they are:** A victim who is looking for emotional support from professionals—counselling, helplines, or peer support—not just reporting or recovery steps.

**Goals:**
- Find someone to talk to (crisis line, support service)
- Reduce isolation and shame; feel heard
- Get signposting to mental health or victim support

**How they use the website:**
- **Emotional support** — `/emotional-support/` — Curated links and numbers for emotional and crisis support (by country where applicable).
- **Get help (nav)** — “Get help” dropdown → “Emotional support.”
- **Immediate help** — `/immediate-help/` — May include support links in the first 0–24 hours.
- **Help now** — `/help-now/` — Country-specific “Need help now?” with support and reporting links.

**Key entry points:** Nav “Get help” → “Emotional support,” links from report thank-you or recovery content.

---

## 6. Family/friend of victim

**Who they are:** Someone whose relative or friend has been (or might be) scammed. They want to help, protect, and guide that person.

**Goals:**
- Recognize signs that someone close is being scammed
- Find ways to talk to the person without confrontation
- Get reporting links, recovery steps, and emotional support links to share
- Protect the relative (e.g. elderly parent) from further harm

**How they use the website:**
- **Learner content** — Scam guides and Stories to understand what their loved one might be going through and which red flags to notice.
- **Report a scam** — `/report/` — If the victim is willing, help them submit a report or do it together.
- **Emotional support** — `/emotional-support/` — Share support links with the victim; get support for themselves if needed.
- **Help now** — `/help-now/` — Official reporting and support by country to pass on.
- **Immediate help** — `/immediate-help/` — Checklist and evidence steps to do with the victim.
- **Tools** — `/tools/` — Protection and recovery tools to recommend or use together.

**Key entry points:** Same as Learner + Emotional support + Report; future “Worried about someone?” or “Help a loved one” block on homepage.

---

## 7. Skeptic / verifier

**Who they are:** Someone who received a message, call, offer, or link and wants to check if it’s a scam before engaging or sending anything.

**Goals:**
- Quickly verify: “Is this a scam?”
- Compare their situation to known patterns (URL, phone, story type)
- Get a clear “what to do” (ignore, report, block)

**How they use the website today:**
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` — Browse by type (phishing, romance, job, etc.) and match their situation to descriptions and red flags.
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Search or browse to find similar experiences.
- **Homepage** — “Top scams at a glance” and “Popular guides” to narrow down the type.
- **Future:** Unified scam checker (paste URL, number, etc.) and search-first flows would serve this persona directly.

**Key entry points:** Homepage guides, Stories, search (when available). Future: prominent “Check if it’s a scam” or “Paste suspicious link/number.”

---

## 8. Recovery-focused victim

**Who they are:** Someone who has already been scammed and wants concrete next steps: report, dispute, freeze accounts, collect evidence, follow a checklist.

**Goals:**
- Report the scam (if not done yet)
- Get an immediate-action checklist (freeze cards, change passwords, 2FA)
- Find dispute and recovery resources (bank, CFPB, identity theft)
- Collect evidence (screenshots, logs) for authorities

**How they use the website:**
- **Immediate help** — `/immediate-help/` — Scam-type-specific checklist and evidence collection; first 0–24 hours focus.
- **Report a scam** — `/report/` — Submit a report and get shareable link.
- **Help now** — `/help-now/` — Country selector → official reporting (FTC, IC3, Action Fraud, etc.) and recovery links.
- **Tools** — `/tools/` — “Recover” section: report fraud, identity theft, credit freeze, etc.
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` — “Where to report” and recovery steps per scam type.
- **Look up report** — `/lookup-report/` — Retrieve their report to share with bank or authorities.

**Key entry points:** Nav “Get help” → “Immediate response,” homepage “Immediate response checklist,” report flow, Help now.

---

## 9. Business / professional

**Who they are:** Small business owner, freelancer, or employee who deals with payments, invoices, or client communications. They want to avoid BEC, invoice fraud, and impersonation.

**Goals:**
- Learn about business-related scams (BEC, invoice fraud, impersonation)
- Get reporting and prevention tips for their role
- Protect business and client data; know official channels

**How they use the website:**
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` — Relevant types (e.g. BEC, phishing, job/employment scams); “Where to report” and “Prepare” sections.
- **Tools** — `/tools/` — Country-specific “Protect & avoid” and “Recover” links; some apply to business context.
- **Report a scam** — `/report/` — Report business-related fraud.
- **Help now** — `/help-now/` — Official reporting links (e.g. FTC, IC3) for their country.
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Filter or read stories about job, invoice, or BEC scams.

**Key entry points:** Same as Learner + Tools; future “For businesses” or “BEC & invoice fraud” entry on homepage or nav.

---

## 10. Educator / community leader

**Who they are:** Teacher, librarian, community center staff, or group leader who wants to run awareness sessions or point their audience (students, seniors, congregation) to trusted resources.

**Goals:**
- Find clear, shareable content (guides, stories, links)
- Get material suitable for different audiences (e.g. seniors, teens)
- Link to official resources so their audience can report and get help

**How they use the website:**
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` — Share specific guide URLs; use content for talks or handouts.
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Share real (anonymized) stories to illustrate red flags and impact.
- **Help now** — `/help-now/` — Official links by country to share.
- **Emotional support** — `/emotional-support/` — Support links for vulnerable audiences.
- **Tools** — `/tools/` — Protection and recovery links to recommend.
- **Newsletter** — `/newsletter/` — Subscribe to get new content to share.
- **About / Contact** — `/about/`, `/contact/` — Understand the site and get in touch for partnerships or materials.

**Key entry points:** Homepage, Stories, Tools, Help now; future “Resources to share” or “For educators” section.

---

## 11. Advocate / return visitor

**Who they are:** Someone who has already used the site (reported, read stories, got help) and wants to stay involved or give back.

**Goals:**
- Stay updated (newsletter, new content)
- Share reports or stories on social to warn others
- Return to report again or recommend the site

**How they use the website:**
- **Newsletter** — `/newsletter/` — Subscribe to get alerts and new guides.
- **Report a scam** — `/report/` — Report again if they see another scam.
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Read and share story links.
- **Share report** — From `/reports/?id=...` use share buttons (Facebook, WhatsApp, etc.) to spread their report.
- **Homepage** — Re-enter via “Report a scam,” “Stories,” or “Subscribe” CTAs.

**Key entry points:** Newsletter CTA, Report a scam, share buttons on report and story pages, return visits from bookmarks or search.

---

## 12. Scam Avenger

**Who they are:** Someone who wants to protect others from scams and fraud—not only themselves. They are motivated to warn people, share knowledge, and reduce harm in the world.

**Goals:**
- Report scams so others are warned
- Share guides, stories, and tips so more people stay safe
- Stay updated (newsletter, new scam types) to spread awareness
- Point friends, family, and community to trusted resources
- Feel part of a “fighting back” effort (aligned with Scam Avenger brand)

**How they use the website:**
- **Report a scam** — `/report/` — Turn their experience (or what they’ve seen) into a public warning; get shareable link.
- **Share** — Use report link, story link, or guide link on social (Facebook, WhatsApp, X, etc.) and in conversations.
- **Stories** — `/stories/` — Read and share real experiences so others can learn.
- **Newsletter** — `/newsletter/` — Subscribe to get new content to pass on.
- **Tools & Help now** — `/tools/`, `/help-now/` — Send links to people who are at risk or already affected.
- **Scam guides** — `/us/scams/[slug]/` — Share specific guides (e.g. romance, phishing) when relevant.
- **Homepage** — Respond to “warn others,” “sharing your experience,” and “Subscribe” messaging.

**Key entry points:** Hero “sharing your experience,” Report a scam (nav and FAB), Newsletter CTA, share buttons on reports and stories. Future: “Become a Scam Avenger” or “Help protect others” block.
